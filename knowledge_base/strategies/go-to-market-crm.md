# Go-to-Market Strategy: CRM for the DACH SME Market

## Starting point

The CRM market in the DACH SME segment is fragmented. Major players (Salesforce, HubSpot) are often too complex or expensive; local vendors have feature gaps.

## Strategic options

### Option A: Niche differentiation
- **Approach**: Industry-specific CRM solution (e.g. trades, healthcare)
- **Advantage**: Less competition, higher willingness to pay
- **Risk**: Limited market potential per vertical

### Option B: Integration platform
- **Approach**: Open API + marketplace for DACH-specific integrations
- **Advantage**: Ecosystem lock-in, partner multiplier
- **Risk**: High development cost

### Option C: Product-led growth
- **Approach**: Freemium model with self-service onboarding
- **Advantage**: Fast scaling, low CAC
- **Risk**: High churn in free tier; SMEs often prefer personal sales contact

## Recommendation

Combination of A + B: Industry-specific modules on an open platform. Start with 2–3 verticals, then expand.
