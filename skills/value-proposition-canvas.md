# Value Proposition Canvas

Deep-dive into product-market fit by mapping customer needs to your offering.

## When to use

- User wants to sharpen product positioning
- User says "value proposition", "product-market fit", "why should customers buy?"
- User is designing a new feature set or repositioning

## Steps

1. **Customer profile** (right side of canvas):
   - **Customer jobs**: What are they trying to accomplish? Functional, social, emotional jobs.
   - **Pains**: What frustrates them? Risks, obstacles, undesired outcomes.
   - **Gains**: What do they want to achieve? Required gains, expected gains, desired gains, unexpected gains.

2. **Value map** (left side of canvas):
   - **Products & Services**: What do you offer? List features and services.
   - **Pain relievers**: How does each product/service address a specific pain?
   - **Gain creators**: How does each product/service create a specific gain?

3. **Fit assessment**: Draw lines between:
   - Pain relievers → Pains (which pains do we actually relieve?)
   - Gain creators → Gains (which gains do we actually create?)
   - Unaddressed pains/gains = opportunities or conscious trade-offs

4. **Prioritize**: Rank pains by severity, gains by importance. Focus on the top 3 of each.

5. **Competitive comparison**: How do competitors address the same pains/gains? Where is our unique fit?

6. **Save**: `wiki/synthesis/vpc-[product].md`

## Output format

```markdown
# Value Proposition Canvas: [Product] for [Segment]

## Customer Profile

### Customer Jobs
1. [Job] (functional/social/emotional)
2. ...

### Pains (ranked by severity)
1. [Pain]: [Description and impact]
2. ...

### Gains (ranked by importance)
1. [Gain]: [Description]
2. ...

## Value Map

### Products & Services
- [Feature/Service 1]
- [Feature/Service 2]

### Pain Relievers
| Pain | Our solution | Effectiveness |
|---|---|---|
| [Pain 1] | [How we solve it] | Strong/Moderate/Weak |

### Gain Creators
| Gain | Our solution | Effectiveness |
|---|---|---|
| [Gain 1] | [How we create it] | Strong/Moderate/Weak |

## Fit Assessment
- **Strong fit**: [Pains/Gains we address better than anyone]
- **Gaps**: [Pains/Gains we don't address]
- **Unique**: [What only we offer]

## Competitive Comparison
| Pain/Gain | Us | Competitor A | Competitor B |
|---|---|---|---|
| ... | ✅ Strong | ⚠️ Moderate | ❌ None |
```

## Pitfalls

- Talk to actual customers, not imagined ones — assumptions kill products
- Jobs are not features — "manage contacts" is a feature, "close more deals" is a job
- Don't try to address every pain — focus beats breadth
- The canvas is per segment — don't mix different customer types
