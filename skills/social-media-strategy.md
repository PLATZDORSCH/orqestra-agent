# Social Media Strategy

Develop a social media strategy aligned with business goals.

## When to use

- User wants to start or improve social media presence
- User asks "social media strategy", "LinkedIn plan", "how should we post?"
- User needs platform recommendations for their business type

## Steps

1. **Assess current state**: What platforms are active? Followers, engagement, frequency?

2. **Define goals**: Brand awareness / Lead generation / Thought leadership / Recruiting / Community. Each goal maps to different metrics.

3. **Platform selection** — recommend based on business type:
   | Platform | Best for | Content type |
   |---|---|---|
   | LinkedIn | B2B, thought leadership, recruiting | Articles, carousels, polls |
   | Instagram | B2C, visual products, employer brand | Reels, stories, carousels |
   | X/Twitter | Tech, news, industry commentary | Short takes, threads |
   | YouTube | Education, product demos, SEO | Long-form video |
   | TikTok | B2C, younger audience, virality | Short video |

4. **Content pillars** (3-4 themes):
   - Educational (how-to, insights, data)
   - Behind-the-scenes (culture, process, team)
   - Industry commentary (trends, news, opinions)
   - Social proof (case studies, testimonials, wins)

5. **Posting cadence**: Recommend frequency per platform. Quality > quantity.

6. **Content formats per platform**: Map formats to the best-performing types.

7. **Draft sample posts**: Create 5-10 example posts the user can adapt.

8. **Save**: `wiki/synthesis/social-media-strategy.md` or `content/drafts/`

## Pitfalls

- Don't be on every platform — better to own 1-2 than be mediocre on 5
- Consistency matters more than frequency
- B2B companies often underestimate LinkedIn — it's the #1 B2B lead channel
- Don't just broadcast — engage with comments and other people's content
- Repurpose blog content into social posts (one article = 5-10 posts)
